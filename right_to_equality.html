Constitution Of INDIA

. Constitution of India (full text)

Article 14 Article 15 Article 16 Article 17 Article 18

Right to Equality

This right ensures that everyone is treated equally by the law. The government and other people must treat you the same way as they treat others, no matter your religion, caste, gender, or place of birth. It has a few important parts:

Equality Before Law: Everyone, whether rich or poor, is equal in the eyes of the law. If two people commit the same crime, they should face the same punishment.

No Discrimination: The government cannot treat people differently just because of their religion, caste, gender, or birthplace. For example, a government job cannot be denied to someone just because of their background.

Equal Opportunities in Jobs: When it comes to government jobs, everyone should have an equal chance. This helps people from all sections of society compete fairly for jobs.

Abolition of Untouchability: In the past, some people were treated as "untouchable" and were not allowed to mix with others. This practice is now completely banned, and treating someone as "untouchable" is against the law.

Abolition of Titles: The government cannot give out titles like “Sir” or “Lord” that make one person seem more important than others. However, military and academic titles, like “Doctor” or “Professor,” are allowed.

It promotes the idea that all individuals, irrespective of their race, gender, caste, religion, or nationality, are entitled to equal treatment before the law. This right prohibits any form of discrimination by the state or individuals, fostering a just society.
